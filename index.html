Our mission

"With a united effort by our team, we hope to tap into the spirit of volunteerism and goodwill - the greatest resource our community offers - to answer the call and help our frontline fighters in the battle for life."

Who we are

We are a collaborative, community-based team protecting frontline healthcare workers by solving the PPE shortage.

Our project began as a response to a design challenge proposed by the NJIT Albert Dorman Honors College for a medically sound, marketable face shield. Seeing our friends and family risking their lives and continuing work without equipment, we knew we needed to help.

What we are doing

We have a design to build a face shield with just a few components. Now we need help assembling it.

We developed a federated, crowdsourced assembly model where drivers deliver materials directly to volunteers - allowing us to produce hundreds of shields and protect hundreds of workers each day.

Safety Guidelines

In order to ensure the safety of everyone involved, there are a few measures that we have decided to take.

Distribution Manager

These are the individuals who will originally receive the supplies in bulk from manufacturers and cut them into kits. When handling these materials managers will ensure exposure to surfaces inside their home is minimal and masks and gloves will be worn at all times. Before handing off supplies to the drivers, the materials will be disinfected via either disinfectant spray, wipes, or natural processes.

Drivers

The delivery drivers will deliver only while wearing a mask and gloves to mitigate any potential contamination of the kits they are distributing to the constructors. Drivers will disinfect their car before putting the supplies in for delivery. In addition to this, we have a strict no-contact policy that requires the delivery driver to make no contact with the constructors and leave the kits at their door or driveway. They will also disinfect the exterior material of the packages for when the fully made face shields need to be picked up, the same no contact policy applies.

Constructors

We would recommend constructors to be cautious and keep the kits outside for an hour or two in the unlikely event that many particles of COVID-19 are on the plastic or cardboard packaging. This measure reduces the risk of a viral load, which would only happen if a driver was without a face mask and coughing or sneezing on the kits. Before starting to work with the kits it is imperative that everyone constructing washes their hands and makes sure any surface they are working on is disinfected. Touching your face, eyes, nose, and mouth should be avoided while handling all of the supplies. The constructors will receive simple and easy-to-follow instructions to make the face shields. After they are finished building their supply of face shields they will place them near their front door to then be picked up by a delivery driver.
